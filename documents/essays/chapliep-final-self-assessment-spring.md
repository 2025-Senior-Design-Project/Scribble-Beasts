My individual contributions were for the back half of round development, as well as
extraneous fixes and quality of life additions. Specifically, I developed the presentation, voting,
and podium rounds. The presentation round allowed the players to display and defend the
characters they created over the course of the game. The voting round allowed the players to rank
their top three favorites from amongst the other players. Finally, the podium round tallied the
points accumulated through voting and displayed the first, second, and third place winners of the
game. I also wrote music to be played throughout the rounds, each slightly different in volume
and character to allow for natural progression in the music.

Our group accomplished a fully playable, ready-to-scale game that is open to anyone to
play, as it is currently hosted online. The aspect of our teamwork that worked was deadlines.
Setting hard and fast deadlines allowed for features to progress as planned with the roadmap, and
missing those deadlines allowed for good accountability and brought natural discussion over why
they were missed. What might not have worked was meeting only once a week. Reflecting over
the semester, a twice weekly check-in might have been more favorable, to ensure our
checkpoints were being hit. Meeting only once a week meant that if something was missing, we
heard about it later than if we had met more frequently.
